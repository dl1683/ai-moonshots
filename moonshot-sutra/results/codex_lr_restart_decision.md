Based on [results/v05_log.txt](C:/Users/devan/OneDrive/Desktop/Projects/AI%20Moonshots/moonshot-sutra/results/v05_log.txt) and [results/codex_training_analysis.md](C:/Users/devan/OneDrive/Desktop/Projects/AI%20Moonshots/moonshot-sutra/results/codex_training_analysis.md), I would stop now and restart `v0.5` at `LR=1e-3`. I would not wait for the 10K eval, and I would not finish the 100K run at `3e-4`.

Reason: the evidence says this run is LR-limited, not unstable. The reported CPU probe shows a double-digit BPT gain from `1e-3`, while the current run is only ~8% into budget and has cost about 2 hours. That sunk cost is small relative to spending the remaining ~90% of training on a known-underpowered schedule. Waiting to 10K mostly increases commitment to the worse trajectory, and cosine decay makes late correction less effective.

I would not rely on a mid-run LR increase for the main production run. Raising LR mid-training is possible, but it is less standard and less clean than restarting because the optimizer state and schedule were shaped by the lower LR and there is no proper warmup into the higher regime. If you want to salvage data, fork the 8K checkpoint and do a short rescue probe with a brief re-warmup to `6e-4` or `1e-3`; but for the actual 100K run, restart clean.
